Tutorial: From Finding to Diagnosis

Dieser Inhalt ist noch nicht in deiner Sprache verfügbar.

In this tutorial you will take a probe finding, write a hypothesis to test whether the pattern is real, and write a diagnosis to explain why it happens. This is the core analytical workflow — the path from “something looks wrong” to “here’s why and what to do.”

Time: 20 minutes Prerequisites: Tutorial: Your First Build completed (you have a working tenant with probe_unbilled_cases)

1. Where we left off

Section titled “1. Where we left off”

After Tutorial #1, you have:

  • A tenant with cases and billing data
  • A probe (probe_unbilled_cases) that finds cases without billing events
  • Findings for CASE_002 and CASE_004

Now the question is: is this a systemic problem, or just noise?

2. Write a hypothesis

Section titled “2. Write a hypothesis”

A hypothesis is a testable business question. Create afs/hypotheses/hyp_billing_gap.yaml:

Key points:

  • evidence references your existing probe with role primary (weight 3)
  • verdict.thresholds set the bar: score >= 0.6 = confirmed, >= 0.3 = plausible
  • interpretation provides human-readable text for all 4 possible outcomes
  • All text is tri-lingual (en/de/fr)

3. Build and check the verdict

Section titled “3. Build and check the verdict”

Open the Explorer:

Navigate to Hypotheses (press H). You should see hyp_billing_gap with a verdict. With 2 out of 5 cases unbilled (40%), and a primary probe with findings, the evidence score should push this to confirmed or plausible depending on the finding count and severity.

Click the hypothesis card to see the detail page: the interpretation text, the evidence chain linking to probe_unbilled_cases, and the probe’s findings.

4. Write a diagnosis

Section titled “4. Write a diagnosis”

Now explain why this is happening. Create afs/diagnoses/diag_billing_process_gap.yaml:

Key points:

  • hypothesis_id links to the hypothesis we just wrote
  • conditions define when this diagnosis triggers (at least 2 unbilled cases)
  • confidence.base is 0.7, boosted to 0.9 if more than 3 findings
  • explanation says why (process failure: missing billing trigger)
  • recommendation says what to do (automate the check)

5. Build and see the full picture

Section titled “5. Build and see the full picture”

Navigate to Hypotheses → click hyp_billing_gap. Below the interpretation, you should now see the Diagnosis section with:

  • Root cause badge: process_failure
  • Confidence bar
  • Explanation text
  • Recommendation text

6. The analytical pyramid in action

Section titled “6. The analytical pyramid in action”

You’ve just built a complete analytical chain:

This is the jinflow method: detect → hypothesize → diagnose → recommend. Each layer is YAML, compiled to SQL, deterministic, and reproducible.

What you learned

Section titled “What you learned”
  • A hypothesis aggregates evidence from probes and produces a verdict (confirmed/plausible/not_observed/insufficient)
  • A diagnosis explains why a confirmed hypothesis is true, with root cause and recommendation
  • The evidence chain is visible in the Explorer: finding → hypothesis → diagnosis
  • All text is tri-lingual — every interpretation, explanation, and recommendation has en/de/fr
